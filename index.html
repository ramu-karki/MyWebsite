Who We Are

A Foundation Built on Compassion

Established in 2014, ACF is Nepal's dedicated cancer support organization — registered under the Organization Administration Act of 1978.

Astha Cancer Foundation (ACF), Nepal is a non-government, not-for-profit social organization founded in 2014 A.D. ACF is registered in the district administration office in Kathmandu, Nepal, committed to fighting cancer through education and community engagement.

ACF focuses on cancer prevention and early detection by partnering with schools, colleges, and social groups to encourage healthier lifestyle decisions that can reduce cancer risk across Nepal.

We also encourage exchange programs with various academic institutions around the world interested in studying cancer in Nepal — incorporating the latest management practices for our patients' benefit.

Purpose

Our Vision & Mission

Guided by a clear sense of purpose — a cancer-aware Nepal where every patient receives timely support and informed care.

Vision
A Society Free of Cancer's Shadow

To enlighten patients and community about early detection and prevention measures of cancer — so that early definitive treatment becomes the norm, leading to a society free from cancer's burden.

Mission
Knowledge as the Cure

To educate cancer patients, their families, and the population as a whole about cancer — its early signs, preventive measures, and pathways to effective treatment available within Nepal and abroad.

Goals

Our Key Objectives

Concrete, actionable goals that drive every program and initiative at Astha Cancer Foundation.

01

Conduct informal education and awareness programs on cancer among diverse communities across Nepal.

02

Provide free counseling services to cancer patients and their family members throughout their journey.

03

Offer free consultation sessions with qualified oncologists for patients who cannot afford specialist care.

04

Conduct regular cancer awareness programs in schools, helping the next generation learn to prevent and detect cancer early.

05

Encourage exchange programs with international institutions for bringing the world's best cancer management practices to Nepal.

06

Provide financial support and medicines to helpless and needy cancer patients who have no other means of assistance.

What We Do

Our Activities

From rural screening camps to international exchanges, our activities span the full spectrum of cancer care and awareness.

  • Educate patients and relatives about all aspects of cancer through IEC (Information, Education & Counseling) materials.
  • Provide proper guidance on options and availability of cancer treatment — both within and outside Nepal.
  • Offer free-of-cost consultations with oncologists for patients who need specialist guidance.
  • Co-ordinate with other cancer support groups to ensure patients receive comprehensive treatment pathways.
  • Provide medicine and financial support to poor and needy cancer patients and their families.
  • Conduct cancer detection and screening camps in both rural and urban areas of Nepal.
  • Counsel patients and family members about early symptoms, preventive measures, and early detection of cancer.
  • Visit communities to educate residents regarding cancer and the treatments available locally and abroad.
  • Conduct regular cancer awareness programs in schools to build a cancer-aware younger generation.
